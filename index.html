Cost Reality Check

Managed hosting is expensive compared to shared hosting — because it replaces labor and reduces risk.

If one preventable outage costs you one retainer month, you’ve already paid for a year of better hosting. Agencies don’t buy “cheap.” They buy less operational drag.

FAQs

Is managed WordPress hosting worth it for agencies?

Often, yes—once you’re managing 10+ client sites. The win is fewer fires: better support, reliable backups, staging workflows, and less time spent debugging hosting issues.

Should agencies use WordPress multisite?

Sometimes, but it adds operational complexity. Many agencies prefer isolated sites for client separation and easier migrations. If you do use multisite, pick a host that supports your workflow and scaling constraints.

How should agencies bill clients for hosting?

Most agencies either bundle hosting into retainers or pass through a predictable line item. Choose hosts with clear plan tiers and understandable scaling so you can price confidently.

What matters most at 10+ sites?

Support competence, predictable scaling, and workflows (staging/backups/migrations). Raw “speed claims” matter less than reliability and fewer late-night emergencies.
